About Me

I am a full stack web developer. After receiving a degree in history, my first passion, I decided to pursue my second passion, programming, professionally. I received a BA in history from the College of William and Mary in September of 2017, then completed a professional studies program at George Washington University. I am a skilled writer; conscientious, organized, and goal-oriented worker. I am well and widely-read and up-to-date on the industry and broader culture. In my spare time, I tutor eighth graders and participate as an adult leader in BSA Troop 624. I am passionate about running, cycling, and reading, especially tech news, favorite blogs, short stories, and horror.


My history thesis was entitled Sino-German Economic Exchanges: 1934-1938, and addressed: German and Chinese politics and geopolitical ambitions; the debate within Germany over a short vs. long war stance; the pro-China vs. pro-Japan lobbies within Germany; German efforts to achieve autarky through a mix of stockpiling/construction of facilities to produce synthetic materials; pre-rearmament policies designed to sustain 'excess' industrial output for later use during wartime; creation and purpose of various front organizations; MEFO bills; interwar monetary vs. fiscal policy; etc. etc.
